Summary

Data on maternal deaths in the U.S. was collected from the CDC Wonder site. Death counts include maternal deaths owing to obstetrical tetanus, maternal deaths up to 42 days after delivery, as well as late maternal deaths (defined by the WHO as the death of a woman from direct or indirect obstetric causes, more than 42 days but less than one year after termination of pregnancy).

Between 2003 and 2017, states were incrementally implementing a pregnancy status checkbox on death certificates which affected the comparability of data between states. By 2018 all states had implemented a checkbox.
